Puerto Rico

Registration Information for Puerto Rico

In Puerto Rico you can register at your local municipality or precinct where you reside. To be eligible to vote you must be a resident of Puerto Rico, and at least 18 years old.

You are ineligible to vote if you have been legally declared mentally incompetent by a court. The identification required to register to vote in Puerto Rico is any from the following: a valid local government issued ID, a valid federal ID, or valid ID, or the last four digits of your Social Security Number.



What else you should know

You can vote early in Puerto Rico if you meet one of the following: Are a member of the Police of Puerto Rico, Officer Corrections, Inmate in a penal institution of Puerto Rico, a member of the Commission, the Secretary, Vice-Presidents and Secretaries, members of local election commissions, and their alternates and affiliate employees, and athlete or member of a sports team, any professional that needs to travel outside of Puerto Rico on Election Day, people confined to a hospital or health care treatment facility, a judge, a person with mobility impairments.

The identification required to vote in person is a valid voter ID card.
